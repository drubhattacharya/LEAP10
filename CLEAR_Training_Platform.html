Phase 4 of 5  ·  Implementation Roadmap

Commitments with
named owners and deadlines.

A strategy without a named owner is a wish. Complete the four components of your implementation roadmap — each requires a specific structural intervention, a real person's name, a deadline, and a mechanism that fires if the target is missed.

The five-element standard for each action
Every action in your roadmap must have: (1) the specific finding it addresses, (2) the structural intervention — not the training, (3) a named individual owner (not a department), (4) a 90-day and 12-month target with a specific metric, and (5) an escalation trigger if the target is missed. Anything missing one of these elements is an aspiration, not a governance commitment.
